# Runbound v0.9.67 — EDNS OPT echo + zero-alloc local-zone lookup

**Date:** 2026-06-03
**Runbound version:** 0.9.67
**Mode:** Bare-metal AF_XDP zero-copy (Drv), Intel X520 (82599) 10 GbE
**Tool:** [dnsmark v1.2.1](https://github.com/redlemonbe/dnsmark)
**Builds on:** [v0.9.66](v0.9.66.md)

---

## What shipped

1. **EDNS OPT echo in the XDP wire fast path** (#156). The hand-rolled wire builder now parses the query's OPT RR and echoes a correct OPT record (advertised UDP payload size) in local-zone A/AAAA answers. The fast path therefore serves **real EDNS traffic** instead of falling back to hickory. Queries with DO=1 (DNSSEC) still fall back to hickory.

2. **Zero per-packet allocation on the local-zone lookup** (#156). `LocalZoneSet` gained a wire-QNAME index (`WireRecordIndex`, CRC32c-keyed via an identity hasher) holding pre-serialised A/AAAA rdata. The hot path lowercases the QNAME in place and looks it up by hash, eliminating the last per-packet hickory allocations that survived v0.9.66: `Name::read`, `LowerName::from`, the `Vec<&Record>`, and the RData dispatch. A round-trip test pins the in-place normalisation byte-identical to the load-side serialisation.

## A/B benchmark — does removing `Name::read` help?

`a.bench.test A`, X520 10 GbE, **`performance` governor pinned**, warm, 5-run medians, back-to-back.

| | v0.9.66 (Name::read present) | v0.9.67 (Name::read removed) |
|---|---|---|
| Throughput (median) | ~6.8 M qps | ~7.2 M qps (**+6 %**) |
| p50 latency | ~0.33 ms | ~0.33 ms (unchanged) |

- The Name::read elimination is a **small but consistent +6 %** throughput in a controlled same-session A/B (the v0.9.67 minimum run sat at or above the v0.9.66 median). p50 is unchanged — the alloc removed was small relative to the per-packet budget.
- As documented in [v0.9.66](v0.9.66.md), **absolute throughput on this shared rig is variance-bound even with the governor pinned** (the same binary ranged ~5.9–7.2 M qps across sessions). The +6 % is the within-session ratio; treat it as "a real but modest per-core gain", not a headline number.
- **The substantive prod win in v0.9.67 is the EDNS OPT echo**, not the +6 %: it widens the fast path from synthetic `+noedns` floods to the EDNS-bearing queries real resolvers send.

## Methodology

Same rig and discipline as [v0.9.66](v0.9.66.md): `performance` governor pinned + warm-up (mandatory — schedutil under-clocks the bursty DNS hot path), `ethtool -N <nic> rx-flow-hash udp4 sdfn`, `ethtool -A <nic> rx off tx off`, `rate-limit: 0`, dnsmark ≥ v1.2.1. Trust medians and p50 latency, never peaks.
